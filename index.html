3. Presentation Skills

Presentation skills is the abilities we use to share our ideas clearly and confidently with an audience. Good presentation skills include speaking clearly, making eye contact, using proper body language, and organizing your thoughts in a way that is easy to follow. Visual aids like slides or charts can help make your message more interesting. It’s also important to stay calm, manage your time well, and respond politely to questions.

4. Group Discussion

G.D. is a form of communication where a small group of people talk about a specific topic or issue. The main goal is to share ideas, understand different viewpoints, and sometimes reach a common conclusion. It helps improve speaking skills, listening skills, teamwork, and critical thinking. In a group discussion, it’s important to speak clearly, listen actively, respect others’ opinions, and stay on topic. It’s not about winning or arguing, but about learning and expressing thoughts in a polite and confident way.

5. 7 C's of Good Writing

The 7 Cs of good writing help us communicate clearly and effectively. They are:

Clarity – Write so your message is easy to understand.

Conciseness – Use only the words you need; avoid unnecessary details.

Correctness – Use proper grammar, spelling, and accurate facts.

Completeness – Include all the important information your reader needs.

Concreteness – Use specific details, not vague or general words.

Courtesy – Be polite, respectful, and positive in your tone.

5. Write about a failure of yours which you consider to share. What have you learned from it?

One failure that stands out in my academic journey happened during my second year of B.Tech. I had taken up a mini-project involving Android app development, something I was very excited about. However, I was overconfident and underestimated the time and planning required. I didn’t break the project into smaller milestones and kept delaying parts of it, assuming I could finish it quickly later. As the deadline approached, I realized I wasn’t prepared. I struggled to fix bugs, and the app was not fully functional by the submission date. I had to submit an incomplete version, which was disappointing and reflected in my evaluation. This experience taught me a valuable lesson about time management and realistic planning. I learned that even if I have the skills, I need a proper structure, consistent effort, and time tracking to succeed. Since then, I’ve started using to-do lists, setting personal deadlines, and breaking tasks into smaller, manageable parts. That failure helped me become more disciplined and proactive in how I handle both academic and personal projects.
